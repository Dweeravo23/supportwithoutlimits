Working With the NDIS

We're in the process of becoming a registered NDIS provider, which means we meet strict government quality and safety standards for disability services.

What is the NDIS?

The National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) is Australia's national scheme for funding reasonable and necessary supports for people with permanent and significant disability.

The NDIS takes a lifetime approach, investing in people with disability early to improve their outcomes later in life.

How We Can Help

Our team can assist you with understanding and accessing the NDIS, from preparing for your planning meeting to implementing your approved plan.

We work with you to ensure your supports align with your goals and NDIS funding.

NDIS Frequently Asked Questions

To become an NDIS participant, you must:

  1. Meet the age requirements (under 65 when you first apply)
  2. Be an Australian citizen, permanent resident, or Protected Special Category Visa holder
  3. Have a permanent and significant disability that affects your ability to take part in everyday activities
  4. Complete an access request form and provide evidence of your disability

Our team can help guide you through the application process.

The NDIS funds reasonable and necessary supports that help you:

  • Achieve your goals
  • Increase your independence
  • Participate in the community and employment
  • Develop skills for daily living

This can include therapies, equipment, home modifications, personal care, community participation, and more. The NDIS doesn't fund supports that are the responsibility of other government systems (like health or education).

Yes! One of the key principles of the NDIS is choice and control. You have the right to:

  • Choose who provides your supports
  • Change providers if you're not happy with the service
  • Manage your own funding if you choose to

We welcome the opportunity to work with you and will respect your choices at all times.

There are three ways to manage your NDIS funding:

  1. NDIA-managed: The NDIA pays providers directly. You can only use registered NDIS providers.
  2. Plan-managed: A plan manager pays your providers for you, helps with budgeting, and keeps track of your funds. You can use both registered and non-registered providers.
  3. Self-managed: You manage the funds yourself, pay providers, and keep records. You can use both registered and non-registered providers.

We can support you with any of these options and help you decide what works best for you.
